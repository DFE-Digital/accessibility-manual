Hearing impairments affect how people perceive and interact with digital content.

Common conditions include:

  • deafness (unable to hear)
  • partial hearing loss (reduced hearing ability)
  • difficulty hearing in noisy environments
  • difficulty understanding speech in noisy environments

Challenges for people with hearing impairments

Hearing impairments can make it difficult to:

  • read small or low-contrast text
  • understand information conveyed by .......alone
  • understand speech in noisy environments
  • understand content when......

Testing tools to identify barriers

Run automated tests

Automated tools can help identify common accessibility issues quickly. While they can't catch everything, they're a good starting point for testing.

You could install and use:

  • axe DevTools
  • WAVE
  • ARC Toolkit

Run manual tests

After automated tests, you should do manual tests next. Manual tests can identify more accessibility problems and can let you see how your product or website works for people with different hearing impairments.

You could check:

  • videos use captions or provide transcripts
  • forms have a linear, logical layout - use headings map to check structure

Testing checklist

You can use an accessibility testing checklist to help you find accessibility issues relating to hearing impairments.

Testing with personas

You could use the GOV.UK accessibility personas to test your product or website. Published in 2017, the personas are a good starting point to understand the user needs of people with disabilities.
