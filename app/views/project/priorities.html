Work with People and Professions

Our approach to accessibility and inclusive design starts with collaboration. We actively engage with diverse groups, including people with disabilities, and work closely with various professions to understand their needs and challenges. This collaborative effort ensures that our solutions are not only compliant but also genuinely inclusive, providing equitable access to everyone who interacts with our services.

Develop Skills, Knowledge, and Awareness

We are committed to continuous learning and development in the field of accessibility and inclusive design. By providing training, resources, and opportunities for professional growth, we aim to equip our teams with the necessary skills and knowledge. Increasing awareness about the importance of accessibility helps us create more inclusive digital experiences and fosters a culture of inclusivity within the Department for Education.

Improve Accessibility and Inclusive Design Culture Across DfE

Building an inclusive design culture is a priority for the Department for Education. We strive to embed accessibility principles into every aspect of our work, from initial planning to final delivery. By promoting best practices and encouraging an inclusive mindset, we aim to ensure that accessibility is a fundamental consideration in all our projects, thereby enhancing the user experience for all individuals.

Create a Sustainable and Scalable Approach to Compliance and Assurance

Our goal is to establish a sustainable and scalable framework for compliance and assurance in accessibility and inclusive design. This involves developing robust processes, leveraging innovative technologies, and ensuring ongoing monitoring and evaluation. By doing so, we can maintain high standards of accessibility and inclusivity, ensuring that our services remain compliant with regulations and are accessible to all users, now and in the future.
