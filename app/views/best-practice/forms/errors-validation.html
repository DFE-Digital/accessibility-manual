When creating forms, error messages are important in alerting users to any information they have missed or mistakes they may have made while submitting their answers.

Good error messages pinpoint exactly what is wrong and provide clear instructions on how to resolve the issues, this reduces confusion and helps to ensure users avoid making the same mistake in the future.

By understanding the error messages provided users are less likely to abandon the form and increases users' confidence on your service.

Using the GOV.UK Design System

Follow the guidance in the design system for correct use of displaying errors in your products. This includes:

  • using the error summary component to summarise all errors at the top of the page
  • using the error message component to display errors next to the form field

Testing error messages

  • get a content designer to 2i your error messages
  • ensure your error messages are associated with the form input containing the error
  • cause an error while using a screen reader, ensure the error is announced via an alert
  • ensure that the error in your summary matches the error next to the form field
