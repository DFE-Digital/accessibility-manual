As a content designer you'll make DfE products and websites easier to understand.

Well structured, clear content helps time-poor and already busy people, such as school business professionals, social workers and nursery managers navigate and use our services.

As a starting point, you should follow the GOV.UK guidance to design accessible services.

Guidance by phase

In discovery

You should:

  • start to plan for a content strategy that focuses on user needs and policy intent. Make accessibility an integral part of the process, to ensure it's always included
  • review guidance for how to meet Service Standard 5

In alpha

You should:

  • explore any offline user journeys and whether there is a phone number or email address for service support
  • review the universal design principles to help you design in an inclusive way
  • consider how disability, age, gender and culture might impact the service, you could use HMRC's accessibility personas
  • write content in plain English and share the Plain Language standard to support your work
  • test different ways to deliver content, to make sure people can perceive content in multiple ways
  • organise content crits to assure your content, the more content is reviewed, the higher chance to identify accessibility issues
  • consider how the service fits with wider DfE services for consistency
  • design and test content with users, including those with access needs
  • start accessibility testingwithin your team

In beta

You should:

  • continue to test with users with access needs
  • consider any non-digital or offline processes and their impact or relation to the service
  • make page title unique and clear
  • check URLs are clear and readable
  • help your team with the wording of the accessibility statement

In live

You should:

  • continue to iterate your service based on insights from user research
  • test changes to make sure they meet accessibility requirements
  • monitor and consider offline processes and their impact or relationship to the journey
