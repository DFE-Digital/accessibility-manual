Disproportionate burden is an important consideration when making services accessible. It recognises that, in some cases, the cost of making specific aspects of a service accessible may be too high relative to the benefit gained.

However, applying disproportionate burden should be done carefully.

Purpose of disproportionate burden

The purpose of disproportionate burden is to provide a balance between the obligation to make services accessible and the practical constraints you may have.

It recognises in some instances, the resources or cost to fix certain accessibility issues might be too high compared to the overall benefit to users.

Follow GOV.UK guidance

To understand and apply disproportionate burden to your product or website follow the guidance on GOV.UKlink opens in a new tab.

Accessibility statements

If you apply disproportionate burden, you must detail this in your accessibility statement.
