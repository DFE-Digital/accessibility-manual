Conformance

An accessibility statement must explain the level of conformance that the website has been tested against. There are three levels of conformance:

  • Level A: The most basic web accessibility features
  • Level AA: Deals with the biggest and most common barriers for disabled users
  • Level AAA: The highest and most complex level of web accessibility

As a department, and widely accepted in other government departments, we aim to meet Level AA, ensuring our digital content is accessible and usable for a wide range of people.

If your website includes features covered by criteria in level AAA, you should aim to meet this level.

Compliance status

By telling people how compliant the website is, you are helping them to understand what adjustments they may need to make. You must declare one of 3 levels of compliance in your accessibility statement.

The 3 levels of compliance

  • Fully compliant: means there are no failures against the level of conformance you have declared.
  • Partially compliant: means between 1 and half of all WCAG criteria have failed to be met.
  • Not compliant: means more than half of the WCAG criteria are not met.

There is legally required wording you must use. Using the correct statement template will ensure this wording is used.

Declaring disproportionate burden

There are situations where you might not be able to resolve an accessibility issue, or it is out of scope. There is guidance on GOV.UK for how to decide if you can use disproportionate burden.
