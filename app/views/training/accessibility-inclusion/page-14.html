You can reduce the chance of excluding people from being able to use a product or service, by including them in the design process.

By putting people at the centre of the design process from the start, and throughout, you can ensure you're desinging things that will work for more people.

People with disabilities can often provide a unique perspective to a problem and by also including people with a diverse background in this process, including age, cultures, ability, and background.

For example

Running co-design sessions with people with diverse perspectives will lead to more innovative solutions that might not be apparent with a less diverse team.

Ensure that this diversity continues through testing and involve users in the design and feedback process. This helps to maintain a product or website that works for more people and is less likely to exclude people.
