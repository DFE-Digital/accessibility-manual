What is accessibility?

Accessibility refers to the design and creation of websites, tools, and technologies so that people with disabilities can use them.

Around 1 in 5 people in the UK have a disability

People with disabilities can have a range of impairments.

Visual impairments

Visual impairments include blindness, low vision, and colour blindness.

People may rely on screen readers, screen magnifiers, or need high-contrast text and designs that do not rely solely on colour.

Hearing impairments

Hearing impairments include deafness or being hard of hearing.

You'll need to providing text alternatives for audio content and ensure that videos with audit contain closed captions or a text-alternative.

Motor impairments

Motor impairments affect movement and coordination, requiring alternatives to a mouse and keyboard.

Adaptive technologies like speech recognition, head or eye-tracking devices can help.

Cognitive impairments

Cognitive impairments include learning disabilities and problems with memory and recall.

Clear, simple layouts, plain language, and tools like text-to-speech can make content more accessible.

Impairments aren't always permanent. They can also occur be temporary, as a result of an accident, or situational, based on an individuals surroundings or environment.
