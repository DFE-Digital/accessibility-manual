People with learning disabilities, memory issues, or cognitive impairments benefit from clear, simple content, consistent navigation, and tools that help with focus and comprehension.

Around half of UK people have some form of cognitive disability.

We should use plain language, avoid jargon, and structure content in a logical and consistent manner. This helps people where English may not be their first language.

For example, writing clear headings, using bullet points for easier readability, and avoiding complex language, and idioms. Using the DfE content style guide and GOV.UK style guide to help write content.
