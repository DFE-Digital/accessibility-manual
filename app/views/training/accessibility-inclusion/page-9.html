Temporary impairments are short-term conditions that affect an individual's abilities for a limited period.

Examples of temporary disabilities include people who have an eye infection, a broken arm or have a head injury or concussion.

Like permanent disabilities, anyone can suffer from one or more temporary conditions at the same time.
