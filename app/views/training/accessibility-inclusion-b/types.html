Accessibility and inclusion

Permanent, temporary, and situational impairments

Not all disabilities or impairments are permanent. Some can be as a result of injury or just the environment the person is in at that time.

Permanent

Permanent disabilities are long-term conditions that affect people throughout their lives.

Examples include blindness, deafness, or mobility impairments.

Temporary

Temporary disabilities are short-term conditions that affect us for a limited period.

These can include things like a broken arm or an eye infection which might mean adapting to how someone interacts with us, our services, products or websites.

Situational

Situational disabilities occur due to specific circumstances or environments.

For example, a person in a noisy environment may temporarily experience difficulties similar to those who are hard of hearing.

There is guidance in the interaction design and content design sections of the knowledge hub, about considerations when designing user interfaces and content.
